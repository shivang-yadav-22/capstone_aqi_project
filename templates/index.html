AQI

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a numerical scale used to communicate the quality of the air in a specific location. It is a measure of how polluted or clean the air is and is based on several major air pollutants, including particulate matter (PM2.5 and PM10), nitrogen dioxide (NO2), sulfur dioxide (SO2), carbon monoxide (CO), ozone (O3), and others. The AQI provides information about potential health effects associated with different levels of air pollution, ranging from good (low risk) to hazardous (high risk).
